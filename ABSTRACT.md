# Abstract

This research examines responsibility drift in AI-mediated systems.

As artificial intelligence systems scale and replicate across decision environments, responsibility often shifts from clearly identifiable human actors toward distributed and opaque system structures. This phenomenon creates structural ambiguity in accountability and governance.

The paper analyzes responsibility drift as a systemic effect emerging from automation, high-replication environments, and complex decision architectures. It proposes a conceptual framework for identifying responsibility nodes and maintaining human judgment stability within AI-mediated systems.

Keywords: Complex Systems, AI Governance, Decision Architecture, Human Judgment, Responsibility Structures
